My story begins with two Nigerian immigrants seeking a life of opportunities for their future children. Both of my parents were educators, instilling in me the thirst to always learn more and generously share my knowledge with others.

Born and raised in the bustling city of San Francisco, California, my two younger brothers and I grew up in a constantly culture-shifting, yet ever-exciting household. From my educational experiences attending a Title I, public elementary school, being the only Black female in a small, private middle school, to immersing myself in the language (both literally and figuratively) of the International Baccalaureate program at a private, French high school- I’ve been exposed to such diverse people, cultures, and ways of thinking. The disparities I came across and have personally experienced have shaped my warm and sharing disposition.

If I have the ability to help others, I will.

Fast-forwarding to my undergraduate years, I studied how the human brain and mind are separate yet complexly entangled entities, and the implications of this entanglement on education and the “institution of learning”.

The brain is plastic and we will all forever be learners. I want to use my skills in people-oriented design, research and problem-solving to increase opportunities for learning in whatever medium or platform that may be. I have a passion for understanding people and their experiences.

I want to find myself in and pursue a career that enables me to build things that contribute to a better, more sustainable, equitable, and pleasant future.
